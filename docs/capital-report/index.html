7 Issues All VCs Agree On

These bottlenecks were independently identified by 4 or more of the 6 investors interviewed

1
Pre-Seed Capital Gap / Angel Investor Shortage 6/6
Portugal has fewer than 6 genuinely active tech angel investors. After 5-6 VCs, founders exhaust the market.
+

"After speaking to 5-6 VCs, you've exhausted the market."

Pedro Almeida, Armilar

"Less than 5-6 real tech angels exist. Everything about Business Angels is artificial."

Stephan Morais

"Portugal needs more angels writing 10K, 15K, 100K checks."

Pedro Bandeira

"Only 2-3 large angel groups plus half a dozen active individuals."

Lurdes Gramaxo, Investors Portugal

"Angel culture still underdeveloped. Non-tech sectors are mostly a desert."

Pedro, Shilling

"You don't need a few large angels. A network of smaller investors, properly coordinated, can mobilize significant capital."

Pedro Telhado, Angel's Way
2
No Fiscal Incentives for Angel Investors 6/6
Portugal offers zero tax incentives for startup investment while real estate enjoys superior fiscal treatment.
+

"Real estate has better fiscal treatment than startup investment."

Pedro Almeida, Armilar

"UK & Israel: 50% tax deduction, losses deductible. Portugal: nothing."

Stephan Morais

"Capital Semente: only EUR 1M invested in 4 years. The 20K cap was absurdly low."

Pedro Bandeira

"There is no predictable, durable incentive system."

Lurdes Gramaxo, Investors Portugal

"We need predictable, stable incentives."

Pedro, Shilling

"High taxation reduces incentives for businesses to operate in Portugal."

Pedro Telhado, Angel's Way
3
Fund-of-Funds Missing or Insufficient 5/6
Portugal Tech I worked but was never continued. Portuguese VCs now raise from Spanish fund-of-funds.
+

"Portugal Tech I launched Indico, Faber, Armilar. Portugal Tech II never happened."

Pedro Almeida, Armilar

"Without a fund-of-funds, the ecosystem has a problem."

Stephan Morais

"Fund-of-funds needed for pension fund diversification."

"The Banco de Fomento fund-of-funds would be very important."

Lurdes Gramaxo, Investors Portugal

"Should have been done earlier. PT funds raise from foreign fund-of-funds."

Pedro, Shilling

No pension funds, insurance companies, or sovereign capital invest in Portuguese VC.
+

"No systematic setup for pension or insurance funds in VC."

Pedro Almeida, Armilar

"Pension funds, fund-of-funds, corporations all need incentives."

"Limited access to institutional LP capital."

Lurdes Gramaxo, Investors Portugal

"Pension and sovereign funds are not exposed to startups. They should be."

Regulatory Burden / Bureaucracy 6/6
Portuguese VC regulation is "extremely harsh" vs Luxembourg/US. Many funds domicile abroad to escape it.
+

"CMVM regulation is complex and expensive. Many funds domicile in Delaware or Luxembourg."

Pedro Almeida, Armilar

"KYC out of control. Banks refuse to deposit VC fund units."

"Capital Semente: the fiscal authority never implemented the mechanism."

Lurdes Gramaxo, Investors Portugal

"Portugal creates 10x more laws than the UK and US."

Pedro, Shilling

"Bureaucracy and administrative complexity make the country less attractive to international investors."

Past Government Programs Mostly Failed 5/6
Direct investment, co-investment programs, and angel syndicates have all had critical design flaws.
+

"PT2020 angel syndicates were bureaucratic. Banco de Fomento calls won by PE."

Pedro Almeida, Armilar

"PRR didn't even ask for track record. Corporate venturing is innovation theater."

Stephan Morais

"600 angels: amateurs who couldn't report. Capital Semente: EUR 1M in 4 years."

Pedro Bandeira

"Startup Voucher was important but bureaucracy ruined it."

Lurdes Gramaxo, Investors Portugal

"Early 2010s angel programs: most died. Political interference created wrong instruments."

Pedro, Shilling
7
Policy Instability / Lack of Long-Term Strategy 4/6
Incentives change every government cycle. No cross-party consensus on startup capital.
+

"Political momentum plus available capital does not equal structural reform."

Pedro Almeida, Armilar

"Incentives must work for both investor and operator."

Pedro Bandeira

"We need a cross-party national strategy outlasting government cycles. Look at Israel."

Lurdes Gramaxo, Investors Portugal

"Peaks followed by droughts. Creates instability and fund breakups."

Pedro, Shilling

7 Reforms That Require No New Money

They require new rules.

💰
1. Angel Tax Incentive (PAITI)
40-50% income tax deduction on angel investments up to EUR 200K/year. Modelled on the UK SEIS/EIS scheme. Make startup investment fiscally competitive with real estate.
🤝
2. Pre-Seed Matching Fund
Government co-investment for pre-seed rounds up to EUR 500K. Match qualified angel investments 1:1 to de-risk early bets and double the effective capital deployed.
🏦
3. Portugal Tech Fund II
Dedicated VC fund-of-funds with track record requirements. Portugal Tech I launched Indico, Faber, and Armilar. A successor is overdue by a decade.
📊
4. Pension / Insurance Pathway
Enable 1-2% portfolio allocation to domestic VC for pension funds and insurance companies. Requires EU Solvency Act revision and national regulatory adjustment.
📜
5. Startup Fund Light + SIFIDE Reform
Simplified CMVM vehicle for smaller funds (<EUR 50M). Reform SIFIDE to allow flexible spending beyond R&D, making VC capital genuinely usable for growth.
🌍
6. Portugal Capital Bridge
International investor delegations paired with regulatory reciprocity. Rebuild the connectivity lost since Web Summit's peak and create systematic pathways for foreign capital.
🎯
7. Corporate Acquisition Incentives
SIFIDE-like M&A deductions for startup acquisitions by Portuguese corporates. Create domestic exit pathways and recycle capital back into the ecosystem.

Methodology

This report synthesizes findings from six in-depth interviews with six leading Portuguese venture capital investors and angel network leaders, conducted between February and March 2026 by Fernando Jardim as part of the 351 Community Leaders Academy Capital Taskforce.

Each interview was semi-structured, lasting 60-90 minutes, covering: the state of capital supply, structural bottlenecks, government program effectiveness, and policy recommendations. Bottlenecks were coded independently and ranked by consensus (number of investors citing each issue).
